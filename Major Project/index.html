Social responsibility is a moral principle of an entity, be it an organisation or individual, to act towards betterment of society at large. It is a duty that every firm and individual should perform so as to maintain the balance between the environment and economy. Their actions should lead towards sustainable development so as to safeguard the interests of the coming generations and at the same time fulfilling the needs of present population.

Benifits

Every adversity, every failure, every heartache carries with it the seed of an equal or greater benefit.

Agricultural livelihoods

Livelihood is defined as adequate stock and flow of food and cash with an individual or a family to meet its basic needs. Livelihood security then means secured ownership of, or access to, resources and income-earning activities, including reserves and assets to offset risks, ease shocks and meet contingencies.

Emergency relief and recovery

Relief is immediate support provided to minimise suffering and provide basic human needs such as food, water and shelter. Recovery is focused on restoring quality of life and community services to pre-disaster levels.

Environmental development

Sustaining the environment is a crucial task for everyone, and companies can contribute through their CSR work. Activities focused on preserving and maintaining the environment are specifically essential for companies known to release large waste, such as energy production and agricultural industries
